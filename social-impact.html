Social Impact Initiatives

Developing programs that address digital divide, algorithmic bias, and ensure equitable access to AI benefits across all communities.

Why This Matters Now

Inequality Amplification

AI concentrates wealth in tech hubs—Global South, rural regions, and marginalized communities systematically excluded.

Algorithmic Discrimination

Hiring, lending, justice, welfare systems encode historical bias—automated discrimination at unprecedented scale.

Surveillance Asymmetry

Privileged populations get AI assistants; marginalized communities face facial recognition and predictive policing.

Representation Crisis

95% of AI researchers from privileged backgrounds—systems designed without input from affected communities.

Our Focus Areas

  • Algorithmic Justice Advocacy — Identifying and remediating bias in high-stakes AI systems
  • Digital Equity Programs — Access, literacy, and agency for underserved communities
  • Community Data Sovereignty — Local control over data collection, use, and benefit-sharing
  • Inclusive AI Development — Diversifying who builds AI through mentorship and career pathways
  • Anti-Surveillance Advocacy — Resisting discriminatory deployment of recognition and prediction tech
  • Global South Partnerships — Centering perspectives from majority-world contexts
  • Disability Justice in AI — Accessibility as design priority, not afterthought

Current Initiatives

Bias Audit Accelerator

Auditing hiring, lending, and criminal justice algorithms for 50+ organizations—remediation frameworks included.

Tech Equity Coalition

200-org advocacy network influencing digital inclusion policy at city, state, and federal levels.

Community AI Co-Creation

Participatory design programs with Indigenous, immigrant, and disability communities building own AI tools.

AI Career Pathways

Mentorship and training programs supporting underrepresented groups entering AI research and industry.
